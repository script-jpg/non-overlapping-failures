We are given the equation \( \frac{3}{2} \div 3 = \frac{x}{10} \), where \( x \) represents the number of pints of water in the next 10 miles. We need to solve for \( x \) and show that it equals 5.
First, simplify the left-hand side of the equation:
\[ \frac{3}{2} \div 3 = \frac{3}{2} \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{3 \times 1}{2 \times 3} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \]
Thus, the equation becomes:
\[ \frac{1}{2} = \frac{x}{10} \]
Next, solve for \( x \) by cross-multiplying:
\[ 10 \times \frac{1}{2} = x \]
\[ 5 = x \]
Therefore, the number of pints of water Jasmine would drink in the next 10 miles is \( x = 5 \).
